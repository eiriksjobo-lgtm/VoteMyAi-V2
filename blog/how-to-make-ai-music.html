HOW TO MAKE AI MUSIC IN 2026

February 19, 2026 · 6 min read

You don't need any musical experience to create AI-generated music. In 2026, AI music generators can produce full songs — with vocals, lyrics, and professional production — from a simple text description. Here's how to get started.

WHAT YOU NEED

Nothing. Seriously. No instruments, no music theory, no software to install. All you need is a web browser and an idea. Every major AI music platform runs in the cloud and has a free tier.

STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE

01
Pick your AI music tool

The three major platforms are Suno (easiest, best for beginners), Udio (best sound quality), and ElevenLabs (best vocals). All have free tiers. We recommend starting with Suno — it has the gentlest learning curve.

02
Write a prompt

Describe the song you want. Be specific: include the genre, mood, tempo, instruments, and vocal style. Example: "Chill lo-fi hip-hop beat with soft female vocals about rainy days in the city, jazzy piano, slow tempo." The more detail you give, the better the result.

03
Generate and iterate

Hit generate and listen to what the AI creates. Most platforms generate 2-4 variations per prompt. Rarely will the first try be perfect — pick the best one and refine it. Try tweaking your prompt, changing the genre, or adjusting the mood.

04
Add custom lyrics (optional)

Most platforms let you write your own lyrics instead of letting the AI generate them. This gives you much more creative control. Write your verses and chorus, paste them in, and the AI will sing them for you.

05
Export and share

Download your track as an audio file. Upload it to YouTube or SoundCloud. Then submit it to VoteMyAI and let the community vote on your creation.

TIPS FOR BETTER RESULTS

Be specific with genres. Instead of "rock song," try "90s grunge rock with heavy distorted guitars, raspy male vocals, and angst-filled lyrics about suburban boredom."

Reference real styles. "In the style of lo-fi bedroom pop" or "cinematic orchestral like a Hans Zimmer score" gives the AI much clearer direction.

Iterate, don't settle. Professional AI music creators often generate 10-20 variations before finding the one they love. Each generation teaches you what works and what doesn't.

Try different tools. A prompt that sounds average on one platform might sound incredible on another. Experiment across Suno, Udio, and ElevenLabs — read our full comparison to learn which tool fits your style.
